About Me

Who I Am

I am a MSc–PhD Dual Degree student in Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur. My research interests lie at the intersection of physics and artificial intelligence — specifically in computational physics, deep learning, computer vision, and high-performance computing.

Currently, I am working on my MSc research project under Prof. Mahendra Kumar Verma, focusing on energy spectra calculations in quantum turbulence and developing GPU-accelerated N-body simulations. I also have strong hands-on experience building ML/DL applications, from medical chatbots using LangChain to railway fault detection using Vision Transformers.

I am passionate about solving complex problems through code and constantly expanding my skill set in modern AI techniques, frameworks, and GPU computing.
